Buying or Leasing Property in Thailand

Own a Property in Thailand

There are many great investment opportunities in Thailand

Are you thinking about putting down roots in Thailand — either permanently or part-time? Whether you’re a digital nomad ready to own a base, a retiree exploring peaceful coastal towns, or an expat seeking a second home, Thailand offers a wide range of property options for foreigners.

🏡 Can Foreigners Buy Property in Thailand?

While foreigners cannot own land directly, you can legally own a condominium (freehold title) and lease land or houses under certain conditions. Here's how most expats invest:

  • ✅ Freehold Condo Ownership — The simplest option for foreigners
  • ✅ Leasehold Property — 30-year renewable leases for villas or land
  • ✅ Thai Company Ownership — Not recommended unless properly structured
  • ✅ Spouse Ownership — For foreigners married to a Thai national

🧭 Popular Places to Buy Property

Thailand’s property market varies a lot by region. Here are top picks for different lifestyles:

  • Bangkok – Urban condos, investment-friendly
  • Chiang Mai – Laid-back mountain life
  • Phuket / Krabi – Luxury villas and beachfront properties
  • Hua Hin – Great for retirees and long-stayers
  • Koh Samui / Pattaya – Vacation rentals and expat havens

⚠️ What to Watch Out For

Before buying, make sure you: - ✅ Use a local property lawyer - ✅ Double-check ownership titles and zoning laws - ✅ Understand foreign ownership limits (e.g., 49% condo rule) - ✅ Know the total taxes, transfer fees, and maintenance costs
